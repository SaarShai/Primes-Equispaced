## Executive Summary
This is not yet ready for submission. The paper contains several genuine strengths, especially the exact Farey exponential-sum identities and the careful computational counterexample, but there are still local mathematical errors, unproved proposition-level claims, and stale internal contradictions that a referee will catch quickly.

The most serious problems are concentrated rather than global: one proof ends with the wrong value, one key equivalence is false as written, two proposition-level ingredients are stated without proof, and parts of the Open Questions section still contradict the paper's own corrected narrative.

## 1. Errors and Contradictions

2. **Critical error: the remark on the correlation ratio states a false equivalence.** The four-term decomposition gives `\Delta W(p) = A - B - C - \mathcal D` and therefore `\Delta W(p) \le 0` iff `B + C + \mathcal D \ge A` ([main.tex:810](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:810)-[837](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:837)). ## 3. Overstatements and Unsupported Claims
- The abstract says the zeta zeros "govern the step-by-step regularity of rational numbers" and that the phase relationship "matches Rubinstein--Sarnak predictions to within `1.1%`" ([main.tex:52](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:52)-[55](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:55)). - The spectroscope section repeatedly sounds stronger than the internal evidence justifies. The paper itself says the substitution of `R(p)` for the Mertens error is empirical ([main.tex:1974](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1974)-[1980](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1980)), so statements about "detecting zeros from Farey data alone" ([main.tex:101](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:101)-[108](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:108)) and providing "independent verification" relative to classical zeta computations ([main.tex:2032](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:2032)-[2036](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:2036)) should be softened.

The figure may show encouraging convergence numerically, but it does not establish consistency in the statistical sense.
- The large-`p` remark says the sign flip at `p=243,799` is a "predictable consequence" of the phase-locked wave crossing zero ([main.tex:1681](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1681)-[1686](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1686)). ## 7. Deep Assessment
- **Is the spectroscope genuinely novel or just the explicit formula applied?** As written, it is closer to an explicit-formula inversion packaged in Farey language than to a new analytic mechanism. The genuinely novel part is the empirical weight `R(p)` extracted from Farey discrepancy and the observation that this geometric quantity seems to carry the same oscillatory information as `M(p)/\sqrt p` ([main.tex:1951](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1951)-[1958](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1958), [main.tex:1974](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1974)-[1986](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1986)). That is interesting, but it is not yet a theorem-level novelty claim.
- **Is the `D(1/p)` proof complete and correct?** Yes, this is one of the cleaner parts of the paper. Proposition [main.tex:1826](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1826)-[1842](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1842) and its proof at [main.tex:1844](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1844)-[1853](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1853) are straightforward and appear correct. The only problem there is notation drift (`g(1/p)` rather than a previously defined symbol).
- **Are the remaining conjectures honest and well-supported?** The shift-squared conjecture is honestly framed because the analytic gap is spelled out ([main.tex:1570](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1570)-[1580](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1580)). The healing conjecture is numerically plausible, but the surrounding prose currently overclaims a `1/3` threshold that the paper's own examples refute ([main.tex:1881](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1881)-[1883](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1883), [main.tex:1901](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1901)-[1904](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1904)). The `R(p)>0` conjecture is not honestly framed in its present wording because the same remark claims the mechanism should eventually make `R` negative ([main.tex:1812](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1812)-[1823](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:1823)). The spectroscope conjecture is best described as a heuristic supported by plots, not as a near-theorem.
- **Is the paper the right length?** No. At roughly 2,400 lines, the paper is too long relative to the amount of fully closed mathematics. The easiest cuts are the broad speculative Open Questions that are far from the core Farey/Mertens story, especially items 6 through 10 ([main.tex:2186](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:2186)-[2259](/Users/saar/Desktop/Farey-Local/paper/main.tex:2259)). The formal-verification and AI-use sections also occupy a lot of space; for arXiv they may stay, but for a mathematics referee report the core paper would be stronger if some of that moved to an appendix or repository README.
