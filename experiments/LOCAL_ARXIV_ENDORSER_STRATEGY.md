### 4. Backup Plan

If you cannot secure an endorser from the initial list after 10–14 days, execute the following steps:

1.  **The "Citation Pivot":** Scour your own bibliography. Identify the 5 most recent authors you have cited. They are the most likely to be interested because they are already familiar with the technical context of your paper.
2.  **Expand the Subject Scope:** If your paper has any combinatorial elements (which Farey sequences often do), search for active authors in `math.CO` (Combinatorics) who have published in `math.NT`. An endorsement from a `math.CO` author is valid for a `math.NT` submission if they have a history of posting to both.
3.  **The "Collaborator Inquiry":** Reach out to any junior researchers or Postdocs you know who are currently active in the `math.NT` arXiv. They are often more responsive to emails and more integrated into the daily arXiv submission cycle than senior professors.
4.  **Direct arXiv Support (Last Resort):** If you have a well-documented history of research but simply lack the "status" for an endorser, you can contact arXiv administrators directly to explain your situation, though this is rarely successful without a single "Yes" from a peer first.
