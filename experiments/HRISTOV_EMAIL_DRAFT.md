# Email Draft: Data Request to Hristov et al.

**To:** Ivan Hristov (ihristov@fmi.uni-sofia.bg) -- corresponding author
**Subject:** Free-group word classification for your 4,860 free-fall orbits (arXiv:2503.00432)

---

Dear Prof. Hristov,

I am writing regarding your impressive database of 4,860 free-fall three-body orbits with high-precision monodromy eigenvalues (arXiv:2503.00432).

We are investigating connections between the algebraic structure of three-body orbit words and dynamical stability, building on the Li & Liao free-group word classification. Specifically, we study how the continued fraction expansion of the fixed point of the associated Gamma(2) Mobius transformation relates to dynamical properties like Lyapunov exponents.

Your database is uniquely valuable because it provides monodromy eigenvalues to 30 correct digits -- the most precise stability data available for free-fall orbits.

We have downloaded and analyzed your syzygy sequence data, but to complete our analysis we need the correspondence between your orbit numbering and the standard Li-Liao topological classification (the free-group words in generators {a, b, A, B} as used by Li & Liao on their GitHub repository).

**Our specific request:**

1. Do you have the Li-Liao free-group word classification for your 4,860 orbits? If so, could you share this mapping?

2. Alternatively, do your orbits overlap with the Li-Liao equal-mass catalog (695 orbits)? If so, which of your orbits correspond to which Li-Liao classes?

This data would allow us to test whether continued-fraction-based invariants of the free-group representation predict your independently-computed Lyapunov exponents -- a result that would establish a novel connection between number theory and three-body dynamics.

We would be happy to share our analysis results and co-author any resulting publication if appropriate.

Thank you for your time, and congratulations on the remarkable precision of your computational work.

Best regards,
