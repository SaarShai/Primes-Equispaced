### **3. How Do These Companies Detect Bearing Faults?**
- **Traditional Methods**:
  - **Vibration Analysis**: Accelerometers measure vibration signals, which are analyzed for anomalies (e.g., increased amplitude, frequency shifts).
  - **Acoustic Emission**: Detects high-frequency noise from micro-cracks or wear.
  - **Oil Analysis**: Checks for metal particles or debris in lubricants.
- **Advanced Techniques**:
  - **Edge Computing**: Real-time data processing at the source (e.g., sensors on equipment).
  - **Cloud Analytics**: Centralized data storage and AI-driven diagnostics.

---

### **4. Algorithms Used for Fault Detection**
- **Signal Processing Algorithms**:
  - **Fast Fourier Transform (FFT)**: Converts time-domain vibration data to frequency-domain spectra to identify fault frequencies (e.g., bearing defect frequencies).
  - **Envelope Analysis**: Detects high-frequency components (e.g., from bearing defects) using band-pass filtering and Hilbert transform.
  - **Wavelet Transform**: Analyzes time-varying frequency content for early fault detection.
- **Machine Learning Algorithms**:
  - **Supervised Learning**: Support Vector Machines (SVM), Random Forest, and Neural Networks (CNN, RNN) for classifying fault types.
  - **Unsupervised Learning**: Clustering algorithms (e.g., k-means) for anomaly detection.
  - **Hybrid Approaches**: Combining traditional spectral methods with AI for enhanced accuracy.
