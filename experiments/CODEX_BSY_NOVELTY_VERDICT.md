# Codex: BSY / Franel-Landau Novelty Verdict
# 2026-04-10 — 24 web searches, comprehensive literature check

## VERDICT: ΔW IS LIKELY NOVEL. NOT a simple rediscovery.

## Key Findings

### 1. ΔW ≠ D(N) - D(N-1)
No canonical equivalence found. ΔW compares two different ordered sets (F_{N-1} and F_N)
with reindexing and insertion corrections. This is structurally different from the
classical Franel-Landau discrepancy of a SINGLE Farey sequence.

### 2. Per-step analysis: NOT standard in the literature
The idea space (mediant insertion, local discrepancy recursions) is classical.
The EXACT per-step observable ΔW(N) looks nonstandard.
Closest: García 2025 (local discrepancy formulas), Huxley 2012 (weighted identities).

### 3. Four-term decomposition: ingredients classical, packaging may be novel
Huxley's weighted identities and García's recursive formulas are related.
The SPECIFIC A-B-C-D split was not found in the literature.

### 4. Prime/composite asymmetry: likely novel observation
"Primes damage, composites heal" not found as a classical Franel-Landau result.
Arithmetic-restricted Farey subsets are studied (Haynes 2003, Welch 2008, BCZ 2004)
but not the SIGNED per-step dynamics.

### 5. Spectroscope: novel as specific scheme, not novel as category
Weighted/Fourier approaches to Farey data are classical.
The M(p)/p prime-weighted spectroscope is not standard.

### 6. BSY is a DIFFERENT result
Balazard-Saias-Yor is about ∫|log|ζ(1/2+it)||²dt, not directly about Farey discrepancy.
Not the immediate threat Opus suggested.

## BOTTOM LINE
"The surrounding ecosystem is classical, but the exact package does not look like
a simple rediscovery of Franel-Landau."

The risk is NOT that the whole program is old.
The risk is that parts may REDUCE to weighted/recursive reformulations of known machinery.

## RECOMMENDATIONS
1. Do NOT describe ΔW as "new formulation of Franel-Landau" (it isn't)
2. Describe as "new per-step weighted Farey observable" built from classical ideas
3. Explicitly separate: RH-equivalent statements / stepwise dynamics / spectroscope / Lean
4. Benchmark against: Franel-Landau, Huxley, García 2025, partial Franel sums
5. The novelty lives in the STEPWISE SIGNED DYNAMICS and the WEIGHTING, not in the Farey/RH background

## CRITICAL TEST (Granville's challenge):
Find one theorem where ΔW succeeds and classical D(N) fails.
If found → novelty confirmed. If not → novelty is in the framework, not the math.
