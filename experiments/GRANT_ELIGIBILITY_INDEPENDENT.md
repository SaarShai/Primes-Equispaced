**Can an independent researcher (no university affiliation) apply for NSF grants, Simons Foundation, AMS grants, and what alternative funding sources exist?**  
Most U.S. funding agencies and foundations **typically require institutional affiliation** (e.g., university, research institute, or non-profit organization) for grant applications. However, **some programs or alternative funding sources** are more flexible for independent researchers. Below is a breakdown of eligibility and five specific alternative funding programs:

---

### **1. National Science Foundation (NSF)**  
- **Typical Requirement**: Institutional affiliation (e.g., university, research institute).  
- **Exceptions**:  
  - **NSF CAREER Award**: Requires a position at an institution (e.g., university, research lab).  
  - **NSF Independent Researcher Grant**: No official program exists for independent researchers; NSF generally funds institutional proposals.  
- **Conclusion**: NSF rarely funds individual researchers without institutional support.  

---

### **2. Simons Foundation**  
- **Simons Fellowship for Early-Career Researchers**:  
  - **Eligibility**: Requires a university or research institution affiliation.  
  - **Focus**: Supports early-career mathematicians (PhD within 5 years).  
- **Simons Investigators Program**:  
  - **Eligibility**: Requires a university or research institution affiliation.  
  - **Focus**: Established researchers with a track record.  
- **Conclusion**: Simons Foundation programs generally require institutional ties.  

---

### **3. American Mathematical Society (AMS)**  
- **AMS Travel Grants**:  
  - **Eligibility**: Open to individuals (no formal affiliation required).  
  - **Focus**: Covers travel to conferences or workshops.  
- **AMS-Simons Travel Grants**:  
  - **Eligibility**: Requires a university or research institution affiliation.  
- **Conclusion**: AMS offers some individual-friendly grants (e.g., travel) but most require affiliation.  

---

### **Alternative Funding Sources for Independent Researchers**  
Here are **five specific programs** with eligibility details for independent researchers (no university affiliation required, or with flexible criteria):

---

#### **1. Guggenheim Fellowship**  
- **Eligibility**: Open to individuals (no institutional affiliation required).  
- **Focus**: Supports advanced research in all fields, including mathematics.  
- **Application**: Nomination by a university, college, or research institution.  
- **Website**: [https://www.guggenheimfoundation.org](https://www.guggenheimfoundation.org)  

---

#### **2. Alfred P. Sloan Foundation Research Fellowships**  
- **Eligibility**: Requires a university or research institution affiliation.  
- **Focus**: Early-career researchers (PhD within 5 years) in STEM fields.  
- **Note**: While institutional affiliation is required, independent researchers may affiliate with a university as a postdoc or visiting scholar.  
- **Website**: [https://www.sloan.org](https://www.sloan.org)  

---

#### **3. Packard Foundation Fellows Program**  
- **Eligibility**: Requires a university or research institution affiliation.  
- **Focus**: Supports early-career researchers in STEM, social sciences, and humanities.  
- **Note**: Independent researchers may partner with an institution for the application.  
- **Website**: [https://www.packard.org](https://www.packard.org)  

---

#### **4. Simons Foundation - Simons Fellowship for Early-Career Researchers**  
- **Eligibility**: Requires a university or research institution affiliation.  
- **Focus**: Supports early-career mathematicians (PhD within 5 years).  
- **Note**: Researchers can apply through their affiliated institution.  
- **Website**: [https://www.simonsfoundation.org](https://www.simonsfoundation.org)  

---

#### **5. National Science Foundation (NSF) CAREER Award (with Institutional Support)**  
- **Eligibility**: Requires a university or research institution affiliation.  
- **Focus**: Early-career researchers (PhD within 5 years) in STEM.  
- **Note**: Independent researchers can apply through a collaborating institution (e.g., as a postdoc or visiting scholar).  
- **Website**: [https://www.nsf.gov](https://www.nsf.gov)  

---

### **Key Takeaways**  
- **NSF and Simons Foundation**: Typically require institutional affiliation.  
- **AMS**: Some grants (e.g., travel) are open to individuals.  
- **Alternative Funding**: Programs like the **Guggenheim Fellowship** and **Sloan Research Fellowships** offer opportunities for independent researchers, though most require a formal institutional connection.  
- **Tips for Independent Researchers**:  
  - Partner with a university or research institute for application.  
  - Explore niche foundations (e.g., Packard, Sloan) or travel grants.  
  - Leverage international funding (e.g., ERC, Humboldt Foundation) if applicable.  

For the most accurate and up-to-date information, always check the official websites of the funding sources.
