The analysis of the four hypotheses in relation to their sufficiency for a resonance proof reveals the following hierarchy from strongest to weakest:

1. **RH (Riemann Hypothesis)**: This hypothesis assumes that all non-trivial zeros lie on Re(s) = 1/2, providing a strong foundation but being unproven.
   
2. **Quasi-RH β ≤ 1/2 + ε**: Allows zeros near the critical line within an epsilon range, offering more flexibility than RH.

3. **Density Hypothesis**: Focuses on the density of zeros near the critical line rather than their exact locations, which is a different measure of weakness.

4. **Proving for the rightmost zero (d)**: This is the weakest as it requires information about only one zero, potentially sufficient if this zero dominates the resonance effect.

**Conclusion**: The weakest hypothesis needed for the resonance proof is proving it for the rightmost zero (option d), as it assumes less and imposes fewer conditions.
