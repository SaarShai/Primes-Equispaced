## VERDICT: NO. Both angles hit fundamental barriers.

## Angle A: Direct contradiction via mean value bounds
- Off-line zeros produce F(γ₀) ~ N^{2β₀-1} → ∞
- K off-line zeros: total mass K·N^{2β-1}
- Zero density: K ≤ CT^{A(1-β)} 
- For contradiction: need A(1-β) + 2β - 2 > 0 → β > 1. But β < 1 always.
- Even density hypothesis (A=2, unproved) gives EXACTLY 0 — borderline, no contradiction.
- WHY: density estimates are CALIBRATED against exactly this argument. Not fixable.

## Angle B: RIP framework
- Lower RIP for K=1 IS the Dichotomy theorem (we already have it)
- But detection ≠ non-existence proof
- "Spectroscope shows nothing" requires checking uncountably many frequencies
- Converting "detects if exists" into "certify absence" = the zero-free region problem

## The fundamental barrier
- Spectroscope: conditional detection (IF zeros exist THEN consequences follow)
- RH: unconditional non-existence (NO zeros exist off the line)
- Bridging requires: exhaustive search (impossible), structural impossibility (= new ZFR), 
  or bootstrap (feed spectroscope into classical machinery — indirect, speculative)
