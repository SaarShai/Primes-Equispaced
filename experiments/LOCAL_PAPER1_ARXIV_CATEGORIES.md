### Endorsement Strategy

If you are a new author or have not submitted to these specific categories recently, you may need an **endorsement**.

1.  **Primary Endorsement Category:** You should seek endorsers in **`math.NT`**. This is the "home" for all three papers. An endorser in `math.NT` is generally recognized across the other related categories (`math.PR`, `math.DST`) in the math-sub-domain.
2.  **Who to ask:** Look for authors who have recently published in the `math.NT` or `math.PR` sections of arXiv and whose work intersects with yours (e.g., researchers working on the Riemann Hypothesis, Montgomery’s pair correlation, or Ergodic theory).
3.  **Note on `math-ph`:** Avoid seeking endorsement in `math-ph` unless your paper is explicitly about a physical system. If you use "spectroscope" as a mathematical tool, you are still a "math" author.
