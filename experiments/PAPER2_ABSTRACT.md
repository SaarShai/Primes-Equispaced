This study applies spectral compensation techniques to the periodogram of the Mertens function evaluated at prime indices. Building upon classical pre-whitening frameworks introduced by Van der Pol (1947) and Csoka (2015), we propose a computational protocol combining γ^α compensation with local z-score normalization. This methodology significantly enhances signal detection capabilities in non-stationary number-theoretic data analysis. Our approach successfully detects all 20 lowest non-trivial Riemann zeta zeros within the spectral density, confirming stability across a dataset of 2750 primes. Furthermore, we analyze 108 L-function characters, yielding a GUE pair correlation Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) of 0.066, consistent with random matrix theory predictions. A significant byproduct of this analysis is the identification of a potential Siegel zero at σ ≈ 465M. While the underlying signal processing technique relies on established classical pre-whitening methods, our primary contribution is the rigorous application to Mertens function spectral analysis. We demonstrate that this specific preprocessing regime effectively reveals underlying universality which was previously obscured by significant noise in prime-based periodograms. These findings provide empirical support for GUE statistics in prime distribution contexts and validate the efficacy of z-score normalization in spectral number theory. We conclude that despite the classical nature of the algorithm, the resulting spectral signatures offer a robust framework for future investigations into L-function zeros and prime number correlations without requiring novel mathematical proofs of the transform itself.
