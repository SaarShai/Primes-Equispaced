This spectroscope computes F(γ) = |∑ M(p)/p · e−iγ log p|² where M(p) is the Mertens function (cumulative Möbius sum) evaluated at each prime. Peaks in F(γ) reveal the imaginary parts of nontrivial Riemann zeta zeros. The γ² matched filter compensates for the 1/γ decay of individual terms, making higher zeros visible. Start with 100 primes (noise), slide to 5,000+ and watch γ1 ≈ 14.13 emerge.
