The Riemann zeta function creates a mathematical landscape along a special path called the critical line. The height at each point encodes hidden information about every prime number.
Every valley — every place the height drops to zero — controls where prime numbers appear. These zeros act like tuning forks, each one shaping the distribution of primes in its own way.
Finding these zeros is one of mathematics' greatest quests. The Riemann Hypothesis says they ALL lie exactly on this line. Nobody has proved it in over 160 years.
We discovered a new way to find these zeros: by listening to the primes themselves. Our Farey spectroscope turns prime spacings into a signal that reveals each zero's location.
