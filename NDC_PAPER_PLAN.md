# Normalized Duality Constant — Paper Plan

## Target
Journal: Mathematical Research Letters (primary), Comptes Rendus Mathématique (backup)
Format: 8-12 page announcement. Conjecture + partial rigorous results + numerics.

## Title (working)
"A normalized duality constant at nontrivial zeros of Dirichlet L-functions"

## Prior art gap (2-3 sentences)
Conrad (Canad. J. Math. 2005) analyzed truncated Euler products at the REAL critical point s=1/2; did not treat complex zeros ρ=1/2+it. Aoki-Koyama (2023) established O(1/log K) rate at zeros but left the multiplicative constant unidentified. Our contribution: couple the Euler product with the Möbius partial sum, observe D_K → 1/ζ(2) universally, verify across 6 characters including complex-order, derive Perron expansion rigorously.

## Paper structure
1. Introduction — conjecture, history, why 1/ζ(2) surprising
2. D_K and its two factors — definitions, contrast with Akatsuka/Conrad
3. Main Conjecture + partial results — formal statement, rigorous vs conjectural
4. Perron expansion for c_K^χ(ρ) — derive ~log(K)/L'(ρ,χ), conditional GRH
5. Dirichlet convolution identity — D_K = 1 + R_K, boundary k∈(K,K²]
6. Numerical evidence — 6 characters, tables, phases, convergence rates
7. Sign contrast with ζ — +1/ζ(2) for L(s,χ), −e^{-γ} for ζ; Koyama DRH dichotomy
8. Open questions — why 1/ζ(2)? squarefree density heuristic; GL(2) extension

## Rigorous vs conjectural
PROVED (conditional GRH): Perron expansion c_K^χ(ρ) ~ log(K)/L'(ρ,χ)
PROVED (unconditional): Convolution identity D_K = 1 + R_K
PROVED (Aoki-Koyama rate): |D_K| = O(1) if limit exists
CONJECTURE: limit exists and equals 1/ζ(2), independently of χ and ρ
